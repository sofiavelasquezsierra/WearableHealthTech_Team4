Why Harmonize?

IMU datasets are often fragmented across studies due to differences in naming conventions, coordinate systems, sampling rates, and metadata structures. These inconsistencies prevent direct integration and limit large-scale analysis. Harmonization transforms heterogeneous datasets into a unified framework, enabling consistent interpretation, dataset integration, and scalable modeling. By standardizing structure and coordinates, it becomes possible to combine multiple datasets and support more robust and generalizable biomechanical and machine learning applications.

6. Coordinate Rotation (ISB Standard)

The system automatically estimates sensor orientation from the signal pattern. It identifies the gravity-related axis using accelerometer magnitude, then uses the remaining axes and gyroscope variation to infer the other directions. A rotation matrix is then constructed to align each sensor to the ISB standard.

Standard: Body Segments (Non-foot)
Target ISB Frame: X = Forward, Y = Up (Gravity), Z = Right
Standard: Foot Segments
